Piper Morgan: An AI Exploration for Product Management

A personal project to experiment with LLMs for product tasks

Christian Crumlish

The Idea Behind Piper Morgan

As the new Product Director, I wanted to explore how LLMs could help streamline routine PM tasks and learn about our unique product context.

My goal: build a "product trainee" service to act as an intern, potentially growing into an entry-level Product Assistant.

Our Iterative Journey So Far

This has been a learning journey, iterating through several distinct sessions:

  • Initial Experiment: Proof-of-concept with OpenAI, answering questions from hard-coded synthetic documents.
  • Functional Iteration: Migrated to Claude Opus, integrated with the Claude API, and developed core capabilities.
  • Current Prototype: "Piper Morgan" can now ingest content, turn plain language into GitHub tickets, and propose revisions for existing tickets.

What Piper Morgan Can Do (Today's Prototype)

It's an early-stage prototype, but it can already:

  • Interpret Natural Language: Understand plain language requests for product tasks.
  • Draft GitHub Tickets: Convert natural language into structured GitHub issues.
  • Suggest Ticket Revisions: Offer comments and proposed edits on existing GitHub issues.
  • Ingest Context: Process and use provided content and categories.

This is a foundational step, not a fully-fledged product yet.

Why This Exploration Matters

Even as a prototype, Piper Morgan hints at significant potential:

  • Efficiency: Streamlining routine documentation tasks for PMs.
  • Consistency: Helping maintain quality and format across tickets.
  • Onboarding Aid: A potential tool for junior team members to learn best practices.
  • Knowledge Leverage: Making internal documentation more accessible and actionable.

It's about amplifying our human capabilities, not replacing them.

Looking Ahead & Open Questions

This is just the beginning. The current prototype has clear limitations:

  • It's a local setup, not a shared service.
  • Learning from user edits is still basic.
  • Further development is needed for robustness and broader adoption.

I'm excited to share this work and gather your feedback as we consider future iterations.
